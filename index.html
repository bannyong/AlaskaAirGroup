Internal Meeting Talking Points

Agenda Item 1 How MuleSoft + Tableau + Informatica + Data Cloud 360 Work Together

The Setup

AAG owns three of four components: MuleSoft (replacing TIBCO), Informatica (replacing Oracle ODI), and Tableau. The missing piece is Data Cloud 360 — the connective tissue that makes the rest of the investment work together.

The Gap: Where D360 Fits

  • Identity resolution at scale — connecting Atmos loyalty IDs, PNR/booking history, contact center interactions, real-time IROPS data, and the DAP's Customer Master into one unified traveler profile.
  • Zero-copy harmonization — references data where it lives rather than duplicating it. Critical for AAG's multi-system estate.
  • Real-time + historical bridge — DAP/MongoDB "hot data" and Databricks "warm data" coexist and are both accessible to downstream consumers simultaneously.

The Architecture Story to Tell

DAP/MongoDB handles operational state → Informatica IDMC pipelines feed Databricks (with CDAM governing access at each layer) → D360 unifies identity across both layers → MuleSoft becomes the primary consumer of the unified profile, distributing it across systems → Tableau visualizes it for decision-makers with governance already enforced.
Key Talking Points
  • D360 complements what they've built in DAP — not a rip-and-replace.
  • Zero-copy means we're not adding to their data duplication problem.
  • The dual-brand requirement is a D360 strength: identity resolution across brands without full consolidation.
  • The medallion architecture they're building in Databricks maps naturally to a D360 semantic layer.
  • CDAM sits across the entire stack — policies defined once, enforced everywhere including Tableau.

Agenda Item 3 What This Unlocks for Tableau

The Actual Problem — Name It Directly

The Tableau barrier at AAG isn't just data fragmentation — it's that governance overhead actively drove teams away from Tableau. The manual process of mapping Databricks access controls onto Tableau was burdensome enough that data services stopped doing it. Analysts responded by keeping logic inside Databricks. This is a named, experienced pain point. Acknowledge it.

The fix isn't telling AAG to try Tableau again. The fix is removing the underlying cause: Informatica CDAM defines access policies once at the Databricks layer and pushes them down automatically to Tableau. No manual mapping. No overhead on data services. The barrier that drove analysts away disappears.

What the Full Foundation Unlocks

  • Governance without overhead: CDAM policies propagate automatically from Databricks to Tableau. Data services gets time back; analysts can trust published data sources again.
  • Clean semantic layer: Informatica IDMC delivers a harmonized gold layer — pre-joined, quality-checked, MDM-enriched. Tableau sits on top of it. Analysts stop asking "which number is right?"
  • Cross-functional reporting: "Guest satisfaction with disruption handling" can pull loyalty (MIDAS), contact center (Salesforce), IROPS (DAP), and resolution (Sabre) from one place — for the first time.
  • Real-time + historical in one view: D360 bridges DAP's operational state and Databricks' history. Tableau dashboards can show both simultaneously.
  • Dual-brand executive analytics: Guest behavior across Alaska and Hawaiian — journey-level performance in one view — because MDM resolved identity across brands.
Key Talking Point
  • Without Informatica, Tableau is limited by fragmentation, ungoverned access, and a missing unified entity model. Informatica removes all three constraints simultaneously. Every dollar AAG has invested in Tableau becomes more valuable — not with a new BI tool, but with the governed data foundation underneath it.

Cloud Data Access Management (CDAM)

Data Governance CDAM Access Control

The Direct Fix for AAG's Tableau Problem

Oz identified the exact problem CDAM solves: the manual overhead of mapping Databricks access controls onto Tableau burdened data services and drove analysts away from published data sources. CDAM eliminates this with no-code policy authoring through Cloud Data Governance and Catalog (CDGC). Policies are defined once and pushed down automatically into Databricks and Tableau simultaneously — no re-engineering, no manual mapping, no overhead.

Metadata-Driven, AI-Powered Enforcement via CLAIRE

CDAM policies use CLAIRE AI to automatically discover, classify, and catalog data across AAG's hybrid estate — Databricks, MongoDB, Sabre, Informatica pipelines. Access controls become contextual and automatic: the right people see the right data based on metadata attributes, regardless of which tool they're using to access it.

Push-Down Enforcement into Databricks & Tableau

Policies can be pushed directly into Databricks and enforced at the platform level, then propagated automatically to Tableau when data is queried. This is the precise mechanism that removes the overhead Oz described. It also supports AAG's PII obligations — CDAM's protection methods (tokenization, redaction, hashing) ensure sensitive guest data (passport numbers, PNR, loyalty profiles) is handled correctly at every layer.

Self-Service Data Access → Frees the Data Services Team

CDAM's integration with Cloud Data Marketplace enables business users in marketing, the contact center, and cargo to request and access governed data products without waiting for engineering. Data stewards and engineers are freed from time-intensive access provisioning to focus on higher-value work — directly addressing the "people are so busy" constraint Robin raised.

Where You Are Today

Alaska Airlines Group has made significant investments in building a modern data infrastructure — and many of those investments are already paying off. But there's a consistent tension that surfaces across every team using Salesforce today — in the contact center, in marketing, in cargo, in corporate sales. It sounds like this:

"We have the system. We just don't have enough of the data."

What your teams are describing is an identity problem. The Customer Master you've built inside DAP is a meaningful start — guest IDs correlated across loyalty, bookings, and contact history. But it's not complete. When a contact center agent answers a call, they can't see the guest's full journey in one place. When a marketing team launches a personalized campaign, they're working from a loyalty segment, not a true behavioral profile. When an AI agent is asked to handle a disruption, it can't act decisively because it can't see the whole picture.

There's a second tension your team has experienced directly: Tableau hasn't fully delivered on its promise. The result is that institutional knowledge and analytical logic lives outside your governed analytics layer.

Both tensions point to the same underlying need: a data foundation that resolves identity, enforces governance automatically, and makes the right data available to every tool and team — without manual effort.
